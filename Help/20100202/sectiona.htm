This question refers only to the child’s hunger level upon arrival at school, rather than throughout the day. 7.  is independent in bathroom habits most of the time

This question refers to the use of a toilet (Western, sit-down toilet).  The child knows when s/he needs to use the bathroom (i.e. does not have “accidents”). The child is able to undo/do zippers, snaps, buttons on clothing that are required to go to the bathroom, can wash and dry hands by him/herself.

 

YES

NO

The child can do all of the above tasks always or most of the time. If a child had an accident once early in the school year answer YES.

 

Also answer YES for children recently exposed to sit-down toilets, who after initial accidents have adapted toileting habits.

The child cannot do most or any of the tasks listed above OR the child has had more than one accident since the beginning of the school year.

 

Also answer NO for children who after at least one year one month of exposure to sit-down toilets continue to have difficulty adapting their toileting habits.
